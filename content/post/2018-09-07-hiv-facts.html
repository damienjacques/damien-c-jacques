HIV facts

Acronyms

  • People Living with HIV (PLHIV)
  • Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)
  • Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)
  • Voluntary Medical Male Circumcision (VMMC)
  • Joint United Nations Programme on HIV/AIDS (UNAIDS)

Definition

  • Incidence: loosely expressed as the number of new cases during some time period.

Facts

90-90-90 targets

  • In 2014, UNAIDS set 90-90-90 targets to be reached by 2020 in order to end the AIDS epidemic by 2030 (UNAIDS, 2014).
  • By 2020, 90% of people living with HIV (PLHIV) will be diagnosed, 90% of those diagnosed will be on antiretroviral therapy (ART), and 90% of people on treatment will achieve viral suppression. This achievement will translate to 73% of PLHIV being virally suppressed (UNAIDS, 2014).
  • Achieving 90-90-90 by 2020 and scaling up to 95-95-95 by 2030 will reduce annual new HIV infections and AIDS-related deaths by 90% compared to 2010 (Stover, 2016).
  • Sweden was the first country to achieve the 90-90-90 target.

Fast Track

  • In 2016, the 90-90-90 targets were extended in the Fast-Track commitments drawn from the 2016 United Nations Political Declaration (UNAIDS, 2016).
  • FastTrack approach goes further than 90-90-90 targets by including rapid scale-up of effective prevention (such as condom programming, pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP), voluntary medical male circumcision (VMMC)) as well as zero discrimination (gender inequality, young peopleâ€¦) (UNAIDS, 2016).

Statistics

  • In 2015, of 63.7 million PLHIV globally, 57% were diagnosed, 46% on ART, and 38% virally suppressed (UNAIDS, 2016)
  • In 2016, 70% were diagnosed, 53% on ART, and 44% virally suppressed (UNAIDS, 2017).
